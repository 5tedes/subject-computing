Internet Safety, Cyber Security & Encryption

Question 1

Which is the best example of a safe social media practice?

Using privacy settings and reviewing tagged posts helps protect your digital footprint and personal information.
Question 2

Which password is strongest?

Strong passwords include upper-case letters, lower-case letters, numbers, and special characters. "Leaf!River7_Hill" has all of these.
Question 3

What is a common sign of phishing?

Phishing emails often use generic greetings and create urgency to trick you into clicking links quickly without thinking.
Question 4

Which is NOT a good password habit?

Reusing passwords is dangerous because if one account is hacked, all your other accounts become vulnerable too.
Question 5

Which measure best reduces malware risk when downloading software?

Always download from official, trusted sources and scan files with anti-malware software before opening them.
Question 6

What is a digital footprint?

Your digital footprint includes all the information about you online from posts, photos, comments, likes, and profiles.
Question 7

What type of malware pretends to be useful software?

A Trojan disguises itself as legitimate software to trick users into installing it, just like the Trojan Horse from Greek mythology.
Question 8

What type of malware locks your files and demands money?

Ransomware encrypts your files and demands payment to unlock them. This is why regular backups are essential!
Question 9

What does a firewall do?

A firewall acts as a barrier between your computer and the internet, blocking unauthorised access and stopping malware from spreading.
Question 10

What type of malware spreads across networks automatically without user action?

Worms copy themselves automatically and spread across networks very quickly without needing the user to do anything.
Question 11

What is a brute force attack?

A brute force attack uses a computer to try thousands or millions of password combinations per second until it finds the right one.
Question 12

What is "shoulder surfing"?

Shoulder surfing is when someone looks over your shoulder to see your password or PIN as you type it in public places.
Question 13

Why are software updates important for security?

Updates patch security vulnerabilities (weaknesses) in software that hackers could use to attack your computer.
Question 14

What does spyware do?

Spyware runs hidden in the background, spying on what you do and collecting passwords and personal information.
Question 15

What is two-factor authentication (2FA)?

Two-factor authentication adds an extra step to verify your identity, like entering a code sent to your phone after your password.
Question 16

What is "catfishing"?

Catfishing is when someone pretends to be a different person online, often using fake photos and information to deceive others.
Question 17

Which of these is NOT a social media platform?

Microsoft Word is a word processing application, not a social media platform. Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat are all social media platforms.
Question 18

How do computer viruses commonly spread?

Viruses attach themselves to programs and spread through email attachments, file downloads, and infected USB drives.
Question 19

What does adware do?

Adware displays unwanted advertisements on your computer. It is often installed alongside free software without you realising.
Question 20

What makes up your digital footprint?

Your digital footprint includes everything about you online: social media posts, photos, comments, likes, profiles, and more.
